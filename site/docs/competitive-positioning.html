Section 03

Moat Articulation

Competitive advantages that compound over time and become harder to replicate as the user base grows.

▲

Actuarial Risk Dataset

Every scan produces structured risk data: agent type, framework, permissions, guardrails present or absent, risk score. More users means more scans. More scans means a proprietary dataset of how real-world AI agents are actually deployed. This becomes the foundation for AI insurance underwriting and benchmark normalization — impossible to replicate without the install base.

COMPOUNDS WITH USERS
⬢

Framework Detection Network Effect

Each new AI framework scanned improves pattern matching for all users. More frameworks supported attracts more developers. More developers contribute new framework detectors. The detection library grows as a community asset. Competitors must replicate years of accumulated patterns, not just the scanning engine.

NETWORK EFFECT
📋

Compliance Template Library

Every new regulation added (NIST AI RMF, ISO 42001, EU AI Act, OWASP Agentic, state AI laws) becomes a compliance template mapped to Agent Shield's risk taxonomy. Each template increases switching cost for GRC teams who have already mapped their agents to controls. Rebuilding compliance mappings is expensive — teams stay.

SWITCHING COST
♥

Open-Source Community Lock-In

The CLI is MIT-licensed. Contributors add framework detectors, compliance mappings, and risk rules. Community contributions deepen the detection surface faster than any funded team can. The open-source community becomes a distributed R&D organization — and the goodwill it generates converts developers into paid customers when their company needs governance.

ECOSYSTEM LOCK-IN
Section 04

Positioning Statement

The single statement that drives every marketing message, sales conversation, and product decision.

For security and compliance teams deploying AI agents who need governance tooling but can't wait 6 months for enterprise procurement, Agent Shield is an open-source audit scanner with transparent pricing that provides instant risk scoring and compliance-ready reports, unlike Oasis and Zenity which require $100K+ contracts and months of sales cycles.

Section 05

Win / Loss Scenarios

Knowing when to fight and when to walk away is the most valuable thing a pre-seed founder can understand about their competitive position.

Competitor
We Win When...
We Lose When...
Oasis Security
Buyer is mid-market or growth-stage. Budget is under $50K. Procurement cycle needs to close in days, not quarters. Compliance mapping is the primary deliverable, not NHI depth.
Buyer is Fortune 500 with existing security platform relationships, a full procurement team, and budget for a multi-year contract. Oasis's Sequoia pedigree matters in that room — ours doesn't yet.
Zenity
Buyer is not a Microsoft shop. They need multi-framework support or are running agents on AWS, Anthropic, or Vercel. Standalone product with clear pricing beats bundled feature in a platform they may not already own.
Buyer is deeply embedded in Microsoft Azure / Copilot Studio and evaluating Zenity as part of a broader M365 security consolidation. Platform bundling and existing relationships will win.
Snyk agent-scan
The need is compliance and audit, not just security scanning. The buyer needs to generate a board report, satisfy an auditor, or map findings to NIST / EU AI Act controls. Snyk gives them CVEs — we give them governance.
Buyer already has a Snyk seat and their security team is satisfied with "good enough" agent coverage. If governance isn't on the agenda yet, Snyk's existing install base and developer trust will beat us every time.
Microsoft AGT
Buyer needs a paid product with SLA, audit-ready PDF reports, a web dashboard, and compliance mappings they can hand to a GRC team or regulator. "Free and I'll figure it out" is not an option for them.
Budget is zero, the buyer is a developer experimenting on a side project, and "free and good enough" is the explicit requirement. Microsoft's free tool sets a floor we can't undercut — nor should we try.
